Ground Combat Roles

Lead Marines in the fight on land — infantry, artillery, engineers, amphibious vehicles, and air defense.

Ground Combat Aviation Combat Combat Support

Ground combat officers lead Marines in the fight on land — from infantry assaults to artillery fires, engineer mobility, amphibious vehicle operations, and air defense. Featured below are primary Military Occupational Specialties (MOS) in ground combat roles.

Infantry Officer

Infantry Officers are central to the role of the Marine Corps as an expeditionary force. They are responsible for training their Marines for every variety of ground combat mission in any environment. They embody the warfighting spirit and lead from the front.

Field Artillery Officer

Field Artillery Officers lead Marines in tactics, gunnery, gun-line drills, communications, maintenance, transportation, and logistics. They provide close-fire support for infantry, armored reconnaissance, and other maneuver elements.

Combat Engineer Officer

Combat Engineers command engineer units whose duties include repair and maintenance of heavy equipment, engineer reconnaissance, obstacle system emplacement and reduction, mine warfare, and construction in support of maneuver forces.

Amphibious Combat Vehicle (ACV) Officer

ACV Officers command amphibious combat vehicle units that transport Marines from ship to shore and in ground maneuver. The ACV is the Marine Corps’ modern amphibious platform, replacing legacy systems in expeditionary operations. They direct units on maneuvers, tactical problems, and combat scenarios.

Low Altitude Air Defense (LAAD) Officer

LAAD Officers command ground-based air defense units that coordinate tactical employment through air command and control agencies, sensors, and other air defense weapons. They evaluate intelligence and deploy fires to protect the force from aerial threats.
